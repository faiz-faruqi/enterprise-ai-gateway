# Compliance Framework Services Agreement

## 1. Parties

This Compliance Framework Services Agreement ("Agreement") is entered into between
RegTech Compliance Solutions Ltd ("Provider") and Meridian Power Holdings Ltd ("Client"),
effective 1 April 2025.

## 2. Contract Value

Annual subscription fee: £95,000, invoiced annually in advance. Professional services for
bespoke framework customisation are charged separately at £280 per hour, capped at
£30,000 per calendar year unless agreed in writing.

## 3. Scope of Services

The Provider shall deliver:

- Continuous regulatory compliance monitoring across REMIT, UK ETS, and CRD IV
- Automated breach detection alerts with 4-hour notification SLA for Category 1 risks
- Quarterly compliance health assessments with board-ready summary reports
- Access to the Provider's regulatory change management platform (cloud-hosted SaaS)
- Annual on-site compliance training for up to 20 Client staff per year

## 4. Term

Initial term: 24 months from the effective date. Automatic renewal for 12-month periods
unless either party provides 90 days written notice of non-renewal.

## 5. Termination Conditions

Either party may terminate:

- For material breach not remedied within 30 days of written notice.
- By the Client for convenience with 90 days written notice, subject to a
  termination fee equal to the remaining monthly subscription fees for the notice period.
- Immediately by either party in the event of insolvency, administration, or liquidation.

The Client may terminate immediately and without penalty if the Provider suffers a
material data security breach affecting Client data and fails to provide a satisfactory
remediation plan within 72 hours of notification.

## 6. Audit Rights

The Client reserves the right to audit the Provider's compliance with this Agreement
on reasonable notice (minimum 10 business days), no more than once per calendar year.
The Provider shall make relevant records, processes, and personnel available for audit.

Third-party audits may be commissioned by the Client at the Client's expense. The
Provider shall cooperate fully and provide access to systems and documentation within
scope.

## 7. Data Protection

The Provider acts as a data processor on behalf of the Client for any personal data
processed under this Agreement. A Data Processing Agreement (DPA) is incorporated by
reference as Schedule 2.

Key obligations:
- Process data only on documented instructions from the Client.
- Maintain technical and organisational measures appropriate to the risk level.
- Notify the Client of any personal data breach within 24 hours of discovery.
- Delete or return all Client personal data within 30 days of contract termination.
- Not engage sub-processors without prior written consent from the Client.

## 8. Service Level Agreement

| Priority | Definition | Response | Resolution |
|----------|-----------|----------|------------|
| P1 — Critical | Regulatory breach detected or imminent | 1 hour | 4 hours |
| P2 — High | Platform outage or delayed reporting | 4 hours | 8 hours |
| P3 — Medium | Non-critical feature issues | 1 business day | 5 business days |

Service credits apply for SLA breaches: 5% of monthly fee per missed P1 response,
capped at 25% of the annual subscription in any 12-month period.

## 9. Confidentiality

All regulatory findings, breach notifications, and compliance assessments produced
under this Agreement are strictly confidential. The Provider shall not disclose Client
compliance status, risk ratings, or breach history to any third party.

## 10. Liability

The Provider's aggregate liability under this Agreement shall not exceed £500,000 or
the total fees paid in the preceding 12 months, whichever is greater.

Neither party is liable for indirect or consequential loss. The exclusion does not apply
to liability arising from wilful misconduct, fraud, or data protection breaches.
